History

Throughout history, depictions of nature have been a recurring theme in various forms of art. In traditional art, landscapes featuring mountains, rivers, forests, and other natural elements have been prevalent. Artists like Thomas Cole, a leader of the Hudson River School, and Caspar David Friedrich, a German Romantic painter, are known for their profound representations of nature in the 19th century.
